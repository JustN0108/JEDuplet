Who Are We?


We're a high school string quartet for hire comprising of two violins, a viola, and a cello focused on delivering high-quality, affordable performances for events such as weddings, ceremonies, or any occasion that requires music.

Why Choose Us?

Affordability

We offer high quality music at rates that make live classical music more accessible.

Quality

All of us are members of the Greater Buffalo Youth Orchestra, an orchestra that is famous across the Greater Buffalo Area for playing music similar in difficulty to professional orchestras. We assure you that you won't be disappointed by our playing level.

Dedication

Each of us has honed our craft for over a decade on our instrument.

Meet the Quartet

Ethan Zhuang

First Violinist

Ethan Zhuang is a Junior at Williamsville North High School. He has been playing the violin for over 9 years, during which he played in the ECMEA All-County Orchestra, the NYSSMA Area All-state Orchestra, and the GBYO/GBYSO orchestras.

Justin Cai

Second Violinist

Justin Cai, a junior at Williamsville North High School, has been playing the violin for over 8 years, where he has been part of many different orchestral groups such as the ECMEA All County Orchestra, the NYSSMA Area All-state Orchestra, and the Greater Buffalo Youth Orchestra. Justin Cai has always been passionate and motivated to continue his musical career.

Ethan Lin

Violist

Ethan Lin is a high school viola player. He has played for 10+ years with experience in the Greater Buffalo Youth Orchestra, along with state and regional orchestras. He is eager to bring his talent to deeply impact the lives of others and bring significant moments to life through music.

Justin Lee

Cellist

Justin Lee is a high school cellist that currently attends Williamsville North High School. Along with being in the symphony honors orchestra at school, he is a member of the Greater Buffalo Youth Orchestra, currently principal cellist, as well as a member of the NYSSMA and ECMEA orchestras. Knowing exactly how beautfiul music can be, he hopes that he can impact his community with his love for the cello.
